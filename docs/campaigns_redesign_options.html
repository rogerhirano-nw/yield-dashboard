UX Proposal

One job per zone — five ways to get there

Every direction fixes the same three problems from the current build: the triage rail overlapping the KPIs, nine co-equal metrics with no hierarchy, and the two campaign deep-dives outranking the portfolio on an “overall” view. They differ in philosophy — calm/editorial vs. dense/operational vs. health-first — so you can react to a feeling, not a feature list. Same real Newsweek tokens throughout; numbers are illustrative.

Hero = Revenue + Pacing only Quality strip = Viewability · Attention · SIVT · GIVT · VCR · CTR Triage = ranked & action-first, never count-walls
1 Editorial Briefing calm · magazine

Leans all the way into the Newsweek editorial voice. The page reads top-to-bottom like a cover story: a one-line “needs you today” briefing bar, then two huge serif hero figures, a hairline quality stat-line (no boxes), and the portfolio as the body copy. No floating rail at all — triage is the lede.

Triage → single ranked briefing bar, top 2 heroes at 58px, quality as inline stats Priority flights → one collapsed strip No fixed rail (zero overlap)

3 Status Board health-first

Answers “is anything on fire?” before anything else. One big verdict banner with a traffic-light reads the whole portfolio in a glance; the ranked triage cards become the hero; KPIs demote to a single quiet stat-line; the table sits one click away. Best if the daily job is exception management, not browsing.

Verdict banner = portfolio health at a glance Triage is the hero, two ranked cards KPIs → one quiet stat-line Table behind “view all”

4 Balanced Split — Work + Watch familiar · fixed

Keeps your current mental model — work on the left, watch on the right — but executes it as a clean, balanced two-column grid that never collides. KPIs are tiered (2 big + 6 small) in the work zone; the watch zone carries ranked triage + PMP signals. Lowest re-learning cost; biggest fix of the actual bug.

Keeps left=work / right=watch model Real grid — no fixed-position overlap Tiered KPIs (2 big + 6 small) Labeled zones

5 Tiles 2.0 evolutionary · lowest risk

The smallest leap from today. Keeps the tile metaphor you like, but introduces hierarchy: two big hero tiles over a row of six small quality tiles, a thin triage ribbon (not a colliding side rail), and the campaign deep-dives demoted to clearly-secondary muted “spotlight” cards. Ships fastest because it reuses your existing tile components.

2 big + 6 small tiles (real hierarchy) Triage ribbon replaces the side rail Campaign cards → muted & secondary Reuses today's components — fastest to ship
